Flat Stanley's Adventures

Exploring the Pacific Northwest for Jacob

Jacob's uncles, Greg & VJ took Flat Stanley with them to explore a few places in the Pacific Northwest. Here are some of the pictures they took as part of their adventure. Clicking the pictures will make them bigger and you can follow the links in the descriptions to learn more about the places they visited.

At the ferry terminal dock

We're on our way to San Juan Island. To get to an island without a bridge, you take a big boat called a ferry. This is the ferry dock where we waited for the boat.

With the tide out

While we waited, we explored the rocky beach. The tide was out so we could see lots of green algae and other plants that grow even when the rocks are underwater.

Driving toward the dock

When the ferry boat arrived, we started driving down the road that would take us onto the boat.

 

 

Pedestrian bridge ahead

To get on the boat, we had to go under the bridge used by passengers who were boarding the boat without cars.

Nearing the ferry boat

As we get closer, you can see how big the boat is.

Towers supporting the ramp

These big towers are needed to hold up the ramp to the boat since it is so heavy. On the ride back, we took a video so you can see how it works.

Driving onto the boat

The ferry boat is so big, it has two decks (like floors in a building) for cars on the sides. The middle is extra tall for trucks and motorhomes. Above the cars are two decks for passengers and then the top deck where the captain and crew sail the ferry.

Guided to a parking spot

One of the ferry crew members guides us to our parking spot on the boat. Once we parked, he put blocks in front of the tires as an extra safety precaution.

Parked and ready to go

Now we’re parked and ready for the boat to leave. We decided to leave the car and head up to the pedestrian decks.

 

 

Other vehicles aboard the ferry

Look at some of the vehicles that are on the boat with us.

Looking out the window as we get underway

When we got up to the passenger deck, we looked out the front window as we set sail.

Outside in the breeze

We decided to walk out onto the outside deck where we could feel the wind as the ferry sailed across the water. The ferry has elevators, bathrooms and a cafeteria as well as lots and lots of seats for passengers.

Seaplane starts its take-off

From the ferry, we saw a seaplane taking off along the water near Friday Harbor. Seaplanes use the water as a runway, so it's part boat, and part plane. Its building up speed for take-off.

Seaplane lifts off from the water

If you look closely, you can see the plane lifting off from the water. When it gets to its destination, it will land on the water.

Officer’s quarters at the American Camp

This is the outside of the officer’s quarters at the American Camp on San Juan Island. There is a British Camp a few miles away. Early in our country’s history, the ownership of the San Juan Islands was disputed. When an American killed a British pig, the "Pig War" began. Luckily, the pig was the only casualty.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Lighthouse with Victoria, Canada across the water

This lighthouse helped keep ships safe as they sailed through the Haro Strait. The hills on the other side of the water are near the city of Victoria, the capital of the Canadian province, British Columbia. Near the lighthouse they had speakers for hydrophones, underwater microphones, so you could hear the Orcas and the boats. Several family groups of Orcas live in the Puget Sound.

Rocky beach and tide pools

The beach on San Juan Island is rocky instead of sandy and the water is very cold. The tides go in an out on a regular cycle based on the moon. When the tides are out, pools sheltering small sea life are left behind.

Up close look at the tide pools

Tide pools have a variety of small plants and creatures that live in them. In this picture, you can see anemone.

 

 

Cargo ship on its way to Vancouver, Canada

The Haro Strait is a busy commercial shipping route. Ships from Asia sail across the Pacific Ocean and bring cargo to the US and Canada.

Up close look at the shipping containers on the cargo ship

If you look closely, you can see the stacks of shipping containers that the cargo ship is carrying. These containers carry all kinds of goods from agricultural products to electronics and even cars.

On the ferry home

We’re back on the ferry on the way home. You can see out the front of the boat as we sail away.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

We’re parked at the front of the ferry

You can see our car (the black one at the front) parked and waiting while we sail to our destination, Anacortes in WA. This video shows our arrival at the terminal.

Boeing factory and runway in Everett, WA

We went to visit the Boeing airplane factory in Everett, Washington. It’s the biggest building in the world by volume, just behind me on the other side of the runway.

Dreamlifter approaching

We just saw the Dreamlifter land. It is a giant plane that holds parts of other planes inside it. Some parts are made in Japan or Italy and then carried here by the Dreamlifter to be assembled.

Up close look at the Dreamlifter

Here you can see the Dreamlifter up close. It may look small in the picture but it is 70 feet tall and 235 feet long - that’s almost as long as a football field!

A robot that builds airplanes

I went inside the Boeing factory with VJ for media day and got to see the big robots they use to make airplane parts.
